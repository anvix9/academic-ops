# Mode: Compare — Side-by-Side Position Comparison

**Trigger:** User says "compare these positions", "which is better", or `/academic-ops compare`

## Workflow

1. **Identify positions to compare** (2-5 positions max):
   - From evaluation reports in `reports/`
   - Or from URLs/descriptions provided by the user

2. **Generate comparison table:**

| Dimension | Lab A (Score) | Lab B (Score) | Lab C (Score) |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Research Alignment | 4.5 | 3.8 | 4.0 |
| Methods Match | 4.0 | 4.5 | 3.5 |
| PI & Group | 4.2 | 3.5 | 4.8 |
| Career Impact | 4.0 | 4.0 | 3.5 |
| Publication Opp. | 3.5 | 4.5 | 4.0 |
| Funding | 4.0 | 3.0 | 5.0 |
| Prestige | 4.5 | 3.5 | 4.0 |
| Location | 3.0 | 4.0 | 3.5 |
| Timeline | 4.0 | 4.0 | 3.0 |
| Growth | 4.0 | 3.5 | 4.2 |
| **TOTAL** | **4.1** | **3.8** | **3.9** |

3. **Qualitative comparison:**
   - "Lab A wins on research alignment and prestige, but Lab B offers better publication opportunities and Lab C has the strongest mentoring signals."
   - Trade-off analysis: what you gain vs. what you give up with each choice

4. **Recommendation:**
   - Clear ranking with reasoning
   - "If your priority is [X], go with Lab A. If [Y] matters more, Lab B is the better choice."
   - Consider the user's career stage: a master's student might prioritize learning, while a postdoc might prioritize publications
