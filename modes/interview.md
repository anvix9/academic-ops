# Mode: Interview — Lab Visit & Interview Preparation

**Trigger:** User says "prepare for interview", "lab visit prep", "interview prep", or `/academic-ops interview`

## Workflow

1. **Read context:**
   - The evaluation report for this position
   - The deep research report (if it exists)
   - `cv.md` — user's CV
   - `research-statement.md` — research interests

2. **Research preparation:**
   - Identify 3-5 key papers from the lab the user MUST read before the interview
   - For each paper: what to focus on, what questions it raises, how it connects to the user's work
   - Identify the PI's current research direction (from latest grant abstracts / recent talks)

3. **Generate interview prep kit:**

### A. Questions They'll Ask You
- "Tell me about your research" — prepare a 2-minute version and a 5-minute version
- "Why this lab?" — specific, not generic (reference papers, methods, questions)
- "What would you want to work on?" — have a concrete project idea that fits their trajectory
- "What are your long-term career goals?" — honest, shows you've thought about it
- Technical questions specific to the methods used in the lab

### B. Questions You Should Ask
- "What does a typical day/week look like for [position level] in your group?"
- "What projects are currently active and which would I contribute to?"
- "How do you approach co-authorship and credit for collaborative work?"
- "What happened to your last [postdoc/PhD student] — where are they now?"
- "What's the lab's relationship with [relevant collaborators/departments]?"
- "How often do you meet with [position level] individually?"

### C. Technical Brush-Up
- List specific methods, tools, or concepts from the position description
- Suggest resources (tutorials, review papers) for quick refreshers
- If the lab uses a specific framework or tool you haven't used, flag it

### D. Research Talk (if presenting)
- Structure: Problem → Your approach → Results → Connection to their work → Future directions
- Duration: typically 20-30 min for postdoc, 10-15 min for internship
- End with a slide connecting YOUR work to THEIR open questions
- Prepare for hostile questions: "Why didn't you try X?" / "This has been done by Y"

### E. Lab Visit Logistics
- Who to meet (other group members, potential collaborators)
- What to observe (lab culture, how people interact, workspace)
- Questions for current students/postdocs (ask them privately!)
  - "What's the mentoring style like?"
  - "How much independence do you have?"
  - "What's the typical publication timeline?"
  - "What do you wish you'd known before joining?"

4. **Save to:** `interview-prep/{num}-{lab-slug}-prep.md`
