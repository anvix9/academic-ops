### Block 1: Position Summary

Stanford NLP Group is seeking a postdoctoral researcher to work on efficient language models for long-context understanding. The position is funded for 2 years through an NSF grant on "Scalable NLP for Document Intelligence." Start date: Fall 2026. Requirements: PhD in CS/NLP/ML, publications in top venues (ACL, EMNLP, NeurIPS), experience with transformer architectures.

### Block 2: Research Alignment Analysis

**Overlap: Very High.** The candidate's PhD thesis on efficient attention mechanisms directly aligns with the lab's focus on scalable NLP. Both the candidate's ACL 2025 paper on sparse attention and the lab's recent work on long-context models address the same core challenge.

**Complementary strengths:** The candidate brings specific expertise in linear-time attention (not the lab's main approach — they've focused on chunked attention), which would diversify the group's technical portfolio.

**Trajectory fit:** This is a natural next step — moving from a PhD focused on efficient attention to a postdoc applying these methods at scale in a top lab.

**Gaps:** The candidate has limited experience with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), which is a growing focus of the lab. This is learnable within the first 6 months.
